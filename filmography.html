Don't Shoot the Puppy

Overwhelmed by her boyfriend's endless niceness, Kate wrestles with guilt through vivid daydreams of puppies, pistols, and shoot-outs.

Breaking up has never felt so cruel as it does for Kate, whose boyfriend Michael is impossibly nice. Paralyzed by guilt, she begins to imagine Michael as a helpless puppy, making the thought of ending things so much worse. Her best friend Beth however, has no patience for Kate's spiraling dramatics and insists she pull the metaphorical trigger. What follows is a whirlwind of noir-inspired fantasies where breakups turn into standoffs, jazz soundtracks swell, and boyfriends bite the dust. Along the way, Kate must learn staying honest with herself doesn't make her a villian; it makes her strong.

A National Film School at IADT Film, starring Rebecca Whelan as Kate, Daisy Moon as Beth, and Max Henderickson as the impossibly nice Michael. Written and Directed by Eve Duffy.

Olfactory Adaptation

A young man educates us about Olfactory Adaptation, becoming so acccustom to something that you hardly notice it anymore.

Ever Wonder why you can't smell your own cologne after 5 minutes?
So does Ben, luckily he knows about Olfactory Adaptation. It's the science behind being so accustomed to something you forget about it's existence, the same way your eyes block out the shape of your nose. As Ben instils us with wisdom from his Bathroom mirror his girlfriend Ciara loiters behind him. The lesson halts and the reality of Ben's situation emerges, the suave Sensai of science Ben is replaced by a timid boy as Ciara berates him.

A National Film School at IADT Film, starring Anand Aliprandi as Ben, and Aria Minoudi as Ciara.
Written and Directed by Eve Duffy.

The Art of Removing the Sticky Bits

A young boy tries to cope with a shifting world as his parents divorce.

This production took place in Leavenworth, Seattle as a part of the film school "Prodigy Camp". I was fortunate enough to be selected as one of 10 filmmakers to study at the school in July 2023. The film stars father and son duo Christian, and Baer Loubek. This film was made possible by all of the hard working staff of Prodigy Camp and has screened across Ireland and New York.
